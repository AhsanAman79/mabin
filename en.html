Who we are

An association that builds bridges

MABIN stands for Main Education and Integration Forum (Main Bildungs- und Integrationsforum e.V.) and was founded as a non-profit association in Frankfurt am Main on 7 March 2008.

Our members are committed people with and without a migration background – first- and second-generation immigrants, students and senior citizens. We share the conviction that integration is best conveyed by those who have walked this path themselves.

  • Participation through one's own strength. Integration means that everyone can take part in all areas of society.
  • Meeting as equals. We bring people together, reduce fears and create understanding.
  • Language as the key. Guidance works best in one's mother tongue – which is why we work multilingually.

Many hands, one community – diversity is our strength.

Our goals

So that integration succeeds

We are convinced that three areas are decisive. They interlock – just like the colours in our logo.

Education

People with a migration background are often disadvantaged – frequently for linguistic reasons. We offset these disadvantages with targeted learning support.

Awareness

Many parents are unfamiliar with the German education system. We explain it – school types, rights and pathways – on request in the mother tongue.

Mediation

Disadvantage often arises from misunderstandings. We mediate between pupils, parents and teachers – so everyone understands each other better.

Faith & community

Faith connects rather than divides

For many people who come to us, faith is an important common denominator. As our community mainly comes from countries shaped by Islam, we offer the opportunity to connect with us through religion – and to pray in our premises.

Since 2015 we have been accompanied by a Muslim imam with an academic background. He highlights the differences between culture and religion and makes clear: faith calls on people to learn the new language, to continue their education and to respect the laws of their new home.
